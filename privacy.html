Privacy Policy

Last updated February 21, 2026  ·  Effective February 21, 2026

Introduction

Ully is an AI-powered coffee companion for baristas and coffee enthusiasts. Responses are generated in real-time by Claude, an AI model by Anthropic.

This policy explains what data we collect, what we don't, and how we handle your information. We collect the absolute minimum necessary to operate the app.

Age Requirement

Ully is intended for users 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe your child has provided information to us, contact support@ullycoffee.com and we will delete it promptly.

What We Collect

The only information we collect and store on our servers is your email address — used solely to create and authenticate your account. We do not use it for marketing.

Everything else — your profile, recipes, saved cafes, and chat history — lives on your device only and never reaches our servers.

What We Do Not Collect

  • Chat messages — stored on your device only, we have no access
  • Photos — sent to Anthropic's API for real-time analysis, never stored anywhere
  • Location — used once per request for weather context, never stored or logged
  • Profile and app data — recipes, cafes, barista follows stay on your device only
  • Date of birth — used for age check at signup only, never stored
  • Analytics, advertising IDs, or any behavioral data
  • Contacts, call logs, browsing history, or any other device data

AI and Generated Content

Ully uses Claude by Anthropic to generate responses and analyze photos. When you send a message or image, it is transmitted to Anthropic's API via a Firebase Cloud Function — your email or account ID is never included. Per Anthropic's API terms, your inputs are not used to train their models. See Anthropic's Privacy Policy for details.

AI responses are informational only and may not always be accurate. Ully is not a substitute for professional advice.

Camera and Photos

Camera and photo library access is requested only for the Dial-in and Scan AI features. You may deny these permissions — the rest of the app will work normally. Photos are never uploaded to our servers or retained after the AI response is returned.

How We Use Your Email

  • Create and authenticate your Ully account
  • Send a password reset email if you request one

We do not send marketing emails. We do not share your email with any third party.

Data Storage and Security

  • Email and credentials stored securely with Google Firebase Authentication
  • All other app data stored locally on your device, never transmitted to our servers
  • We maintain no database of user-generated content, recipes, or conversations

Third-Party Services

  • Google Firebase — Authentication only. Stores your email. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Anthropic Claude API — Processes AI messages and photos in real-time. Not used for training. Subject to Anthropic's Privacy Policy.
  • wttr.in — Weather data via approximate location. No account or identifier transmitted.

We do not use analytics SDKs, advertising networks, crash reporting, or any other third-party tracking tools.

We Do Not Sell Your Data

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information for commercial or marketing purposes. This applies to all users, including California residents under CCPA.

Your Rights

  • Access the email linked to your account
  • Edit your local profile at any time in the app
  • Delete your account and all data from within the app
  • Clear your local chat history at any time
  • Request deletion by email if you can't access the app

California residents (CCPA) and EEA/UK residents (GDPR) have additional rights. Contact support@ullycoffee.com to exercise them.

Account Deletion

Delete your account at any time from the Profile tab in the app. See Account Deletion for step-by-step instructions. Email requests are processed within 30 days.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated within the app. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
